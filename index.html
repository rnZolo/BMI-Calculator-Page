Limitation of BMI

Although BMI is often a practical indicator of healthy weight, it is not suited for every person. Specific groups should carefully consider their BMI outcomes and in certain cases, the measurement may not be beneficial to use.

Gender

The development and body fat composition of girls and boys vary with age. Consequently, a child's age gender are considered when evaluating their BMI.

Age

In aging individuals, increased body fat and muscle loss my cause BMI to underestimate body fat content.

Muscles

BMI may misclassify muscular individuals as overweight or obese, as it doesn't differentiate muscle from fat.

Pregnancy

Expectant mothers experience weight gain due to their growing baby. Maintaining a healthy pre-pregnancy BMI is adviseble to minimise health risks for both mother and child.

Race

Certain health concerns my affect indidviduals of some Black and Asian origins at lower BMIs than others. To learn more. It is advised to discuss this with your GP or practice nurse.
